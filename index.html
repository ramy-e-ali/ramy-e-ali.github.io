I received the PhD degree in electrical engineering from Penn State University, University Park, USA, in 2020. Before that, I received the M.Sc. degree in wireless communications from Wireless Intelligent Networks Center (WINC), Nile University, Giza, Egypt, in 2014 and the B.Sc. degree in electrical engineering from Alexandria University, Alexandria, Egypt, in 2011.

I was a research intern at Osram Sylvania (Siemens Business), Beverly, MA, USA in 2015. I was a research intern at the Math of Communications department, Algorithms, Analytics & Augmented Intelligence group, at Bell Labs, Crawford Hill, NJ, USA in 2017 and 2018. I was also a research intern at the IP Networking department, E2E Network & Service Automation (ENSA) group, at Bell Labs, Murray Hill, NJ, USA in 2019.

I received the Young Innovators Award (YIA) in 2011, the Nile University Graduate Fellowship in 2011, the Pennsylvania State University Graduate Fellowship in 2015, the Newport Research Excellence Award in 2016 and the Unix 50 Presentation Award, Bell Labs in 2019.

My research interests include distributed systems, information theory, coding theory, reinforcement learning and wireless communications
