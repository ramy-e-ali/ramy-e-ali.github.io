About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Southern California working with Prof. Salman Avestimehr. I currently work on distributed computing, secure and private machine learning. Beside that, my research interests also include distributed systems, information theory and wireless communications.

Education

I received the Ph.D. degree in electrical engineering from Penn State University, University Park, PA, USA, in 2020. Before that, I received the M.Sc. degree in wireless communications from Wireless Intelligent Networks Center (WINC), Nile University, Giza, Egypt, in 2014 and the B.Sc. degree in electrical engineering from Alexandria University, Alexandria, Egypt, in 2011. In my PhD, I studied the fundamental limits and developed code constructions based on erasure coding for strongly consistent key-value stores. In addition, I studied the latency-consistency trade-off of eventually consistent key-value stores.

Awards

I was a recipient of the Young Innovators Award (YIA) in 2011, a Nile University Graduate Fellowship in 2011, a Pennsylvania State University Graduate Fellowship in 2015, the Newport Research Excellence Award in 2016 and the Unix 50 Presentation Award, Bell Labs in 2019.
